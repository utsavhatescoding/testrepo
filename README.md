# 📊 My Project

## 📝 Title
**Studying The Impact of COVID-19 on Banking Stability in BRICS Countries**

---

## 📄 Abstract
Using a panel of publicly listed commercial banks across the BRICS economies, this study examines the spillover effects of the COVID-19 pandemic on bank stability from 2013 to 2024.

Key findings:
- Significant negative structural break in bank stability during COVID-19
- Stability did not fully recover to pre-pandemic levels
- Monetary and regulatory policies improved stabilization
- Profitability decline was the main driver of instability

---

## 📌 Introduction
The COVID-19 pandemic triggered one of the most severe global economic downturns in modern history.

- Massive capital outflows (~$100 billion)
- Strong impact on emerging economies
- High vulnerability in BRICS banking systems

### 🎯 Research Objectives
- Analyze impact of COVID-19 on bank stability
- Examine structural break during pandemic
- Compare recovery across BRICS economies

---

## 📚 Literature Review
Key insights from previous studies:

- COVID-19 reduced profitability and stability (Elnahass et al., 2021)
- Increased risk and volatility in banks (Tran et al., 2022)
- Government intervention played a stabilizing role
